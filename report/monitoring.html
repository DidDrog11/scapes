Monitoring SCAPES progress and sampling

Author

David Simons

Status

Data is being collected from 9 villages across 3 states.

52 households have currently been sampled between the period of 2023-12-08 and 2023-12-21. These households have been sampled in 1 village(s). These village(s) are Zugu - Benue.

Sample characteristics

Household size

The sampled households include 351 individuals with a median of 6 individuals per household and an interquartile range (IQR) of 4. This is slightly higher than the mean number of household members reported in the Nigeria DHS 2018 report of 4.3 (urban) and 5 (rural), however, based on the reported summary statistics this is not normally distributed and so the median may be more similar. Further, there may be important heterogeneity by region in Nigeria. Primary data access to the DHS to allow better comparisons has been requested.

Distribution of household sizes

Sex

160 females (0.46) and 191 males (0.54) are permanently or temporarily resident within these households. This compares to a 1.06 male/female ratio at birth (CIA World Factbook) and data from the DHS 2018 which suggests that women are more likely to reside in rural (54.2%) than urban (45.8%) settings and more so than males (53.6% rural vs. 46.4% urban).

Age

The sampled households have a median age of 16 (IQR = 25) which compares to a median age of 19.2 (CIA World Factbook). Females within the households sampled have a median age of 19 (IQR = 25) which compares to 19.4 for females from the CIA World Factbook. Males within sampled households have a median age of 14 (IQR = 25) which compares to 18.9 for males from the CIA world Factbook.
